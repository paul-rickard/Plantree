The first slice of the **work-order module**: the contracts and worked examples

## The contracts

| Schema | Entity | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| [`schemas/work-order.schema.json`](../../schemas/work-order.schema.json) | **WorkOrder** | The costable unit of work â€” one document embedding tasks, labour, parts, failure report, the content **snapshot**, and the audited **transition history**. |
| [`schemas/work-request.schema.json`](../../schemas/work-request.schema.json) | **WorkRequest** | Reported demand before commitment; triaged and either rejected or **converted** into a work order. |
| [`schemas/workflow-profile.schema.json`](../../schemas/workflow-profile.schema.json) | **WorkflowProfile** | The lifecycle expressed as **data**: ordered states + guarded transitions. `WorkOrder.status` is a state key; a `WorkOrderType` may point at a profile. |

Together these make the [lifecycle](../../docs/architecture/05-work-management-lifecycle.md)
executable: the profile defines the legal states and moves, and every work order
carries a complete, audited `statusHistory` â€” closed and rejected states are
terminal and immutable.

## The two entry paths

A work order reaches the machine two ways â€” which is why `WorkRequest` and
`WorkOrder` are separate entities:

- **Converted from a request** â€” enters at `requested`, goes through
  review/approval (demand needs triage). See
  [`../work-requests/wr_ups_fan_noise.json`](../work-requests/wr_ups_fan_noise.json)
  â†’ [`wo_rm_ups_fan.json`](wo_rm_ups_fan.json) (a reactive `RM` order, converted
  and worked through to `completed` with a failure report).
- **Generated by a schedule** â€” enters directly at `planned` (the strategy is
  already approved), carrying a **snapshot** of the pinned job-plan version. See
  [`wo_pm_generator_weekly.json`](wo_pm_generator_weekly.json) (a `PM` order from
  the Standby Generator weekly schedule, referencing
  [`acp_standby_generator`](../asset-class-plans/acp_standby_generator.json)).

## Deliberately standalone (for now)

Following the same scope discipline as job plans, cross-module references are held
as keys/free text until those modules exist:

- `assetId`, `scheduleId`, `assignedTo.id`, `partRef`, permit and failure codes
  are opaque keys â€” no `Asset`, `MaintenanceSchedule`, `Part`, `Permit` or code
  taxonomy records are required yet.
- Reserve-then-consume is modelled on `partsUsage` (`reservedQty` / `consumedQty`)
  but does not yet move real stock.
